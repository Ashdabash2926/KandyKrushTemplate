14. Care of Your Balloons
To keep your balloons looking good for as long as possible, please follow the guidance below.
14.1
Temperatures affect balloons. When moved from a room temperature environment into the cold, the foil will deflate slightly. Once back indoors the foil should return to fully inflated. Heat may expand the balloon and possibly cause it to burst.
14.2
Do not keep your balloons in the car or garage as temperature cannot be controlled.
14.3
Keep balloons away from direct sunlight and warm places. Helium expands in warm temperatures so balloons can burst if left in front of windows, in conservatories, cars etc.
14.4
Avoid very low temperatures as they can affect the float time of the balloon. Balloons prefer comfortable room temperatures.
14.5
Keep the balloons away from open windows, doors and breezy spots as they can be blown against sharp objects and will burst.
14.6
Please handle balloons with extreme care as they are delicate. It is best to keep little fingers away from the balloons as they are not built to be played with (at least until the party is over!).
14.7
Make sure balloons are not near any sharp objects. Check there are no nails in walls or sharp corners/edges close to where you are displaying the balloon(s) as they will puncture if they brush against them.
